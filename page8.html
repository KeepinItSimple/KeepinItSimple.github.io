KEEPING THINGS SIMPLE

I started creating with my great grand father in his shed. It was filled to the brim with scraps and tools.
When I saw him building a Weather vane out of an old bicycle axle and some metal sheets from an old lawnmower.

I knew that was what I wanted to do.
In my eyes he was an inventor he showed me there where other ways to do things. He is my biggest hero and my biggest inspiration.

R.I.P. Frans I hope to make you proud...

To study art was the most natural thing to do. I felt compelled to kinetic art right away.
I recognised the ways of my great grandfather in Panamarenko, Tinguely, Gerrit Van Bakel and fell in love with the inventions of da vinci, ideas of Duchamp and many more.

I started building my collection of machines that had "a function without use" A walking chair, elevator without a way to leave other that the bottom floor,
a machine that worked on crappy clay gears that stamps crappy clay gears to repair itself etc...

Summer break filled with student parties? No thanks.
I spent it on the attic in isolation, drinking and sleeping whenever, soon I lived during the night
bothering on one. Even tho this lifestyle wasn't very productive I loved it!

As an "upcomming artist" I found the exentric lifestyle suited me and just went with it
But I have to admit the drinking was becomming a problem... 

Luckely at the end of the summer break I had a hiking trip planned. Me and my dad went to walk the west highland way of scotland.
Here I sobered up again, regained a proper routine realized how important health and a clear mind is to be the best we can be.
I rediscovered the beauty of nature again.

After this trip I was creating one of those sculptures and it dawned on me that the beauty I found in nature lied within the function and practicality of it all.
Everything in nature has a purpose, anything else just won't cut it. Raw functionality like an oiled machine.

I realized how complex we live life today and the contrast of the simplicity back in the woods. We are so distand from nature. Inspired by this realization I started to make a collection of furniture allong my sculptures based on these principles.

Simple and functional. No fuzz! Using raw materials nature offered, wood and iron to serve as a reminder and bring us closer again, bound by the straight lines and structure we love to create.
